# GriefPrevention Player Guide

This server uses the **GriefPrevention** plugin to protect player builds from griefing, stealing, and unwanted changes.

This guide explains:
- What claims are
- How to create and manage claims
- How to give other players access
- Commands you can use

---

# Command Quick Reference

| Command | What It Does |
|---------|--------------|
| `/claimslist` | Shows your claims and available claim blocks |
| `/trust <player>` | Allows a player to build and interact in your claim |
| `/containertrust <player>` | Allows a player to open containers but not build |
| `/accesstrust <player>` | Allows a player to use doors, buttons, and switches |
| `/untrust <player>` | Removes permissions from a player |
| `/untrust all` | Removes permissions from everyone |
| `/abandonclaim` | Deletes the claim you are standing in |
| `/abandonallclaims` | Deletes all your claims |

---

# What Is a Claim?

A **claim** is a protected area of land that belongs to you.

When you create a claim:

- Other players **cannot break your blocks**
- Other players **cannot place blocks**
- Other players **cannot open your chests**
- Other players **cannot steal items**
- Your builds are **protected from griefing**

Only **you** control what happens inside your claim unless you give permission to others.

Think of a claim like **owning land** in the world.

If land is **not claimed**, anyone can modify it.

---

# Claim Blocks

Claims use **claim blocks** to determine how much land you can protect.

You gain claim blocks by:

- Playing on the server
- Server rewards
- Admin events

You can check your claim blocks using:

`/claimslist`

This command shows:
- Your claims
- Available claim blocks
- Claim sizes

---

# Creating Your First Claim

To create a claim you need a **Golden Shovel**.

## Steps

1. Hold a **Golden Shovel**
2. Right-click the **first corner** of the land
3. Walk to the **opposite corner**
4. Right-click again

The area between those two points becomes your claim.

Your land is now protected.

---

# Viewing Claim Borders

Claim borders show where your protection begins and ends.

To see them:

- Hold a **Golden Shovel**
- Right-click inside your claim

Markers will appear showing the corners of your claim.

---

# Expanding or Resizing a Claim

You can make your claim bigger later.

## Steps

1. Hold a **Golden Shovel**
2. Right-click a **claim corner**
3. Move to the new location
4. Right-click again

The claim will expand or shrink depending on where you place the new corner.

You must have enough **claim blocks** available to expand.

---

# Giving Other Players Access

By default **only you** can interact with your claim.

You can allow other players access using trust commands.

---

## Full Trust

Command: `/trust <player>`

Allows the player to:

- Build blocks
- Break blocks
- Open containers
- Use doors and switches
- Fully interact with the claim

Use this for trusted friends or teammates.

---

## Container Trust

Command: `/containertrust <player>`

Allows the player to:

- Open chests
- Use furnaces
- Use crafting tables
- Use buttons and levers

Does NOT allow:

- Placing blocks
- Breaking blocks

This is good for shared storage or farms.

---

## Access Trust

Command: `/accesstrust <player>`

Allows the player to:

- Open doors
- Press buttons
- Use switches

Does NOT allow:

- Opening containers
- Building or breaking blocks

This is useful for visitors.

---

# Removing Permissions

To remove a player's permissions:

`/untrust <player>`

To remove **all players** from your claim:

`/untrust all`

---

# Deleting a Claim

If you no longer want a claim, stand inside it and run:

`/abandonclaim`

This deletes the claim and returns the claim blocks.

To delete **all your claims**:

`/abandonallclaims`

Use this carefully.

---

# Subclaims (Advanced)

Subclaims are smaller protected areas inside a main claim.

They allow different permissions in different parts of your base.

Examples:

- Public farm inside your base
- Shop area
- Shared storage room

## Creating a Subclaim

1. Hold a **Stick**
2. Mark two corners inside your claim

The area becomes a **subclaim**.

Subclaims inherit permissions from the main claim unless changed.

---

# Why Claims Are Important

Claims protect players from:

- Griefing
- Item theft
- Base destruction
- Players modifying builds without permission

Always claim important builds such as:

- Houses
- Storage rooms
- Farms
- Villager areas
- Redstone builds

---

# Tips for Using Claims

Good habits:

- Claim your base early
- Leave space between claims
- Only trust players you know
- Check claim borders before building

Avoid:

- Claiming land you do not use
- Blocking other players from expanding
- Giving trust to strangers
